Article I

The terms, stated plainly

  1. One script. Zero dependencies.

    A single ES5 file that runs everywhere, injects its own styling, and calls no third-party servers. Your visitors' data never leaves your page to ask whether it may be collected.

  2. No dark patterns.

    Accept and decline carry equal weight. Granular per-category toggles, a re-open link for changed minds, and re-consent when your policy actually changes. Consent that would hold up in a conversation, not just in court.

  3. Proof, not promises.

    Every fresh decision gets a unique consent ID and a standardised audit record you can log to your own endpoint. Built for GDPR Article 7: the burden of proof is yours, so the receipts are too.

  4. Speaks five languages.

    English, Dutch, German, Spanish and French built in, with full text overrides if you would rather phrase things your own way.

  5. Configured in minutes.

    A config generator produces your settings and embed snippet. Google Tag Manager integration sets consent defaults to denied before anything loads.

  6. The lowest price, guaranteed.

    A flat price per site. No pageview meters, no subpage limits, no features held hostage in higher plans. Your traffic growing is good news, not a billing event. Find a comparable paid consent manager for less, and we beat that price by 10 percent. In writing. Full terms in Article IV.

Article V

A few fair questions

Isn't there a free cookie banner?

Yes, and for a simple site a free tool can be enough. The catch is usually in the details: free tiers tend to limit you to one domain or a fixed number of subpages, put the vendor's name on your banner, and keep the consent record or its export behind a paid plan. With PlainConsent the proof, the five languages and your own branding are included from the first euro, at a flat price with no per-pageview meter. If a free tool covers what you need, use it. If you want provable consent that looks like your own brand, that is us.

Do I even need this if I do not track anything?

If your site sets no non-essential cookies, you may not need a consent banner at all, and we will happily tell you so. PlainConsent is for when you do run analytics or marketing tags and have to ask first, then prove you asked.
